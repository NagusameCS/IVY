## Solving Equations

## Overview

This lesson focuses on mastering the techniques for solving various types of equations, including linear, quadratic, and simultaneous equations. Students will develop a strong understanding of algebraic manipulation and apply these skills to solve problems within the context of functions.

## Key Concepts

*   **Equation Solving:** The ability to determine the value(s) of the variable(s) that satisfy a given equation.
*   **Inverse Operations:** Using operations that 'undo' each other (addition/subtraction, multiplication/division) to isolate the variable.
*   **Linear Equations:** Equations where the highest power of the variable is 1.
*   **Quadratic Equations:** Equations where the highest power of the variable is 2.
*   **Simultaneous Equations:** Systems of two or more equations with the same variables.

### Linear Equations – One Variable

> **Important:** The goal when solving linear equations is to isolate the variable on one side of the equation.

Let's start with solving simple linear equations with one variable.  This builds a fundamental understanding of algebraic manipulation.

**Example 1:**

Solve the equation: $3x + 5 = 14$

**Step 1:** *Subtract 5 from both sides* to eliminate the constant term on the left side.
$3x + 5 - 5 = 14 - 5$
$3x = 9$

**Step 2:** *Divide both sides by 3* to isolate 'x'.
$\frac{3x}{3} = \frac{9}{3}$
$x = 3$

**Solution:** $x = 3$

**Check:** Substitute $x = 3$ back into the original equation: $3(3) + 5 = 9 + 5 = 14$. The equation holds true.

**Example 2:**

Solve the equation: $5 - 2y = 10$

**Step 1:** *Subtract 5 from both sides*
$-2y = 10 - 5$
$-2y = 5$

**Step 2:** *Divide both sides by -2*
$y = \frac{5}{-2}$
$y = -\frac{5}{2}$ or -2.5

**Solution:** $y = -\frac{5}{2}$

**Check:** Substitute $y = -\frac{5}{2}$ back into the original equation: $5 - 2(-\frac{5}{2}) = 5 + 5 = 10$. The equation holds true.

**Example 3:**

Solve the equation:  $2(x - 1) = 8$

**Step 1:** *Distribute the 2*
$2x - 2 = 8$

**Step 2:** *Add 2 to both sides*
$2x = 8 + 2$
$2x = 10$

**Step 3:** *Divide both sides by 2*
$x = \frac{10}{2}$
$x = 5$

**Solution:** $x = 5$

**Check:** Substitute $x = 5$ back into the original equation: $2(5 - 1) = 2(4) = 8$. The equation holds true.

### Solving Quadratic Equations

> **Exam Tip:**  Quadratic equations can be solved using various methods, including factoring, completing the square, and the quadratic formula.  Understanding the quadratic formula is essential for HL students.

**Example 4:** (SL - Factoring)

Solve the quadratic equation: $x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0$

**Step 1:** *Factor the quadratic expression*
$(x - 2)(x - 3) = 0$

**Step 2:** *Set each factor equal to zero and solve for x*
$x - 2 = 0 \implies x = 2$
$x - 3 = 0 \implies x = 3$

**Solution:** $x = 2, x = 3$

**Example 5:** (HL - Quadratic Formula)

Solve the quadratic equation: $2x^2 + 3x - 2 = 0$

**Step 1:** *Identify a, b, and c*
$a = 2$, $b = 3$, $c = -2$

**Step 2:** *Apply the quadratic formula*
$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$
$x = \frac{-3 \pm \sqrt{3^2 - 4(2)(-2)}}{2(2)}$
$x = \frac{-3 \pm \sqrt{9 + 16}}{4}$
$x = \frac{-3 \pm \sqrt{25}}{4}$
$x = \frac{-3 \pm 5}{4}$

**Step 3:** *Calculate the two possible values for x*
$x_1 = \frac{-3 + 5}{4} = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}$
$x_2 = \frac{-3 - 5}{4} = \frac{-8}{4} = -2$

**Solution:** $x = \frac{1}{2}, x = -2$

### Solving Simultaneous Equations

> **Note:** Simultaneous equations involve solving two or more equations with the same variables. Techniques include substitution and elimination.

**Example 6:**

Solve the following simultaneous equations:

$y = 2x + 1$
$3x + y = 11$

**Method 1: Substitution**

**Step 1:** *Substitute the expression for 'y' from the first equation into the second equation*
$3x + (2x + 1) = 11$

**Step 2:** *Simplify and solve for x*
$5x + 1 = 11$
$5x = 10$
$x = 2$

**Step 3:** *Substitute the value of x back into either equation to solve for y*
$y = 2(2) + 1$
$y = 4 + 1$
$y = 5$

**Solution:** $x = 2, y = 5$

**Check:** Substitute $x = 2$ and $y = 5$ into both original equations:
*   $y = 2x + 1 \implies 5 = 2(2) + 1 \implies 5 = 5$ (True)
*   $3x + y = 11 \implies 3(2) + 5 = 6 + 5 = 11$ (True)
