**Problem:** Solve for the values of x when the corresponding set of y's is contained in a circle centered at the origin in Cartesian space (the circle has radius 1). This problem demonstrates an application of recurrences to real-world problems and highlights the importance of understanding these relationships.

By doing this, we obtain:
y_n = xt^n = (R - x)(xt + x)... (an infinite series), where T is the term that sums to 1.
Thus, by the definition of a recurrence relation, we have:
x_0 = R(x) and y_k ≠ x_{n-2} for n > 1
