Lesson Introduction
=================

Introduction to Fermat's Little Theorem:
Fermat's Little Theorem is a fundamental result in number theory, which states that if p is a prime number and a is an integer not divisible by p, then a^(p-1) ≡ 1 (mod p). This theorem has numerous applications in mathematics and computer science.

Basic Concepts
=================

Key concepts to include:

1. A fundamental theorem in number theory
2. What does Fermat's Little Theorem mean? It is a rule that states that for any integer not divisible by another integer, the greatest common divisor (GCD) of the smaller integer and the larger integer is equal to their GCD.
3. The significance of Fermat's Little Theorem in number theory: it provides an explanation of how numbers behave as they increase modulo a prime number.
4. Its role in cryptography (especially RSA): Fermat's Little Theorem plays a crucial role in public-key cryptography, such as RSA encryption and digital signatures, by ensuring that messages cannot be factorized into their prime factors.
