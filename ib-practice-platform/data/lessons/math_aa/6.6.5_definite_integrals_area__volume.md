Title: Definite Integrals, Area & Volume

### What is an integral?
Intuition-driven solutions to problems and a deeper understanding of how we can find areas and volumes with calculus help students deepen their grasp on math concepts.

#### 6.6.5 - Calculus
1. **Defining areas**
    - Recall the concept of area as a measure or quantity that applies to shapes with bounded volume, such as cubes, spheres, rectangles, and trapezoids.
    - Introduce "definite integrals" (denoted by d), which can be thought of as the process of finding an antiderivative of a definite function.
2. **Area vs Volume**
    - Focus on area; students see that areas are related to bounded volume; they also learn how to find volumes, using the formula for the surface area of a cube (SA).
3. **Interpreting results**
    - Ask questions like "How do definite integrals relate to other math concepts?" and "What specific result can we use in our solution." This helps students understand that areas are not just about accumulating volume; they also provide insight into how we might apply calculus to solve problems involving volumes or finding the area of shapes.
4. **Real-world applications**
    - Discuss what definite integrals reveal about real-world phenomena, such as calculating the area under a curve in physics (frictionless surfaces), determining the volume of objects made from various materials, or optimizing performance within systems like circuits and computers.
5. **Interactive exploration**
6. **Example:**  Solve for areas of rectangular prisms by integrating the product of their lengths with respect to one diagonal length, which is a classic problem in geometry.
