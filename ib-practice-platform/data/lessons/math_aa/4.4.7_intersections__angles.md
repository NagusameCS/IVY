## Key Concepts
*   Students will learn to calculate the angles between vectors using the dot product.
*   Understanding the geometric interpretation of the dot product is crucial for solving problems involving angles and vector relationships.

### Subtopic 1: The Dot Product – A Quick Review

The **dot product** (also known as the scalar product) of two vectors, **a** and **b**, is a scalar quantity. It’s defined as:

```
a ⋅ b = |a| |b| cos θ
```

Where:

*   |a| and |b| are the magnitudes (lengths) of vectors **a** and **b** respectively.
*   θ is the angle between the two vectors.

> **Important:** The dot product is *not* commutative.  In general, a ⋅ b ≠ b ⋅ a. This is a crucial point for students to grasp.

**Example 1:**
Given **a** = <1, 2> and **b** = <3, -1>, calculate a ⋅ b.

1.  Calculate magnitudes: |a| = √(1² + 2²) = √5 and |b| = √(3² + (-1)²) = √10
2.  Apply the formula: a ⋅ b = (√5)(√10) cos θ = √50 cos θ
3.  Since a ⋅ b = 1*3 + 2*(-1) = 3 - 2 = 1, then 1 = √50 cos θ
4.  cos θ = 1/√50
5.  θ = arccos(1/√50) ≈ 1.4286 radians or 81.37°

### Subtopic 2: Calculating Angles Between Vectors Using the Dot Product Formula

The dot product formula provides a direct way to calculate the angle θ between two vectors.  Let's break down the steps.

**Steps:**

1.  Calculate the magnitudes of both vectors, |a| and |b|.
2.  Substitute the magnitudes and the angle θ into the dot product formula:  a ⋅ b = |a| |b| cos θ.
3.  Rearrange the formula to solve for cos θ: cos θ = (a ⋅ b) / (|a| |b|)
4.  Calculate cos θ.
5.  Find the angle θ by taking the inverse cosine (arccos or cos⁻¹) of cos θ.  Ensure your calculator is in degree or radian mode as appropriate.

**Example 2:**
Given **a** = <2, -1, 3> and **b** = <1, 0, -2>, find the angle between them.

1.  |a| = √(2² + (-1)² + 3²) = √14
2.  |b| = √(1² + 0² + (-2)²) = √5
3.  a ⋅ b = (2)(1) + (-1)(0) + (3)(-2) = 2 + 0 - 6 = -4
4.  cos θ = (-4) / (√14 * √5) = -4 / √70
5.  θ = arccos(-4/√70) ≈ 1.905 radians or 109.47°

### Subtopic 3:  Vector Projections and the Angle Formula

The angle between vectors can also be derived from the concept of **vector projection**. The projection of vector **a** onto vector **b** (denoted as proj~b~**a**) is the component of **a** that lies along **b**.

The formula relating the angle θ to the vector projection is:

```
proj_b a = (a ⋅ b) / |b|² * b
```

This means that the length of the projection is given by the dot product scaled by the squared magnitude of **b**.

**Example 3:**
Let **a** = <1, 1> and **b** = <1, 1>.  Find the angle between them.

1. a ⋅ b = (1)(1) + (1)(1) = 2
2. |b|² = 1² + 1² = 2
3. proj_b a = (2 / 2) * <1, 1> = <1, 1>
4. Since **a** and **b** are equal, the angle between them is 0 degrees (or 0 radians).  This can be verified with the dot product formula: a ⋅ b = |a| |b| cos(0) = (1)(1) * 1 = 1, which confirms the result.

### Subtopic 4:  Applications in 3D Space

The same principles apply in 3D space.  Vectors are represented as ordered triples (x, y, z). The dot product becomes:

```
a ⋅ b = aₓbₓ + a_y b_y + a_z b_z
```

The angle θ calculation follows the same steps as outlined previously.  *Exam Tip:* Always double-check your calculations in 3D space to avoid sign errors, as the dot product is sensitive to the order of the vectors.

### Subtopic 5:  Solving Word Problems

Often, problems will present scenarios involving vectors and angles. The key is to translate the given information into a mathematical representation using the dot product.

**Example 4:**

A force of 10N is applied to a block at an angle of 30° to the horizontal. A second force of 5N is applied horizontally. Find the angle between the two forces.

1.  **Represent the forces:** Let **F1** be the force of 10N at 30° to the horizontal, and **F2** be the 5N force applied horizontally.  We can express these as:
    *   **F1** = <10cos(30°), 10sin(30°)> = <8.66, 5> N
    *   **F2** = <5, 0> N

2.  **Calculate the dot product:**  **F1** ⋅ **F2** = (8.66)(5) + (5)(0) = 43.3 N

3.  **Calculate magnitudes:** |**F1**| = √(8.66² + 5²) = √84.3 = 9.18 N, |**F2**| = 5 N

4.  **Apply the formula:** cos θ = (43.3) / (9.18 * 5) = 0.491
5.  **Find the angle:** θ = arccos(0.491) ≈ 60.5°
