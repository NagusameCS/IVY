## Lesson: Radian Measure, Arc & Sector

## Overview

This lesson introduces students to radian measure, a fundamental alternative to degree measure in trigonometry. We'll explore the relationship between radians and degrees, learn how to convert between them, and then investigate the concepts of arcs and sectors, connecting them to trigonometric functions.

## Key Concepts

*   Understanding **radian measure** as a unit of angular measurement.
*   Converting between **degrees** and **radians**.
*   Calculating the **arc length** and **area of a sector** using radians.
*   Applying these concepts to solve problems related to circular geometry.

### 3.3.1.1 Introduction to Radian Measure

> **Important:** Degree measure is based on a 360° scale, while radian measure is based on the radius of a circle.  This fundamental difference leads to a more direct connection between angles and the circumference of the circle.

Radian measure defines an angle as the ratio of the arc length subtended by the angle at the center of a circle to the radius of the circle. Let's denote this angle as θ.

*   **Arc Length (s)** = radius (r) * θ (in radians)
*   **Relationship between Radians and Degrees:**  π radians = 180° and 2π radians = 360°

### 3.3.1.2 Converting Between Degrees and Radians

Converting between degrees and radians is a crucial skill. Here’s a quick reference:

*   **Degrees to Radians:**  radians = degrees * (π / 180)
*   **Radians to Degrees:** degrees = radians * (180 / π)

**Example 1:** Convert 60° to radians.
```
radians = 60 * (π / 180)
radians = π/3 radians
```

**Example 2:** Convert π/4 radians to degrees.
```
degrees = (π/4) * (180 / π)
degrees = 45°
```

**Example 3:**  A central angle in a circle measures 75 degrees. What is the radian measure of this angle?
```
radians = 75 * (π / 180)
radians = 5π / 12 radians
```

### 3.3.1.3 Arc Length

The **arc length (s)** of an arc of a circle with radius *r* subtended by an angle θ (in radians) is given by:

$$s = rθ$$

This formula directly relates the arc length to the radius and the angle in radians.

**Example 4:**  A sector of a circle has a radius of 10 cm and a central angle of π/6 radians. What is the length of the arc?
```
s = rθ = 10 * (π/6) = 5π/3 cm
```

### 3.3.1.4 Area of a Sector

The **area (A)** of a sector of a circle with radius *r* subtended by an angle θ (in radians) is given by:

$$A = \frac{1}{2}r^2θ$$

This formula provides a way to calculate the area of a sector directly from the radius and the angle in radians.

**Example 5:** A sector of a circle has a radius of 8 m and a central angle of π/3 radians. What is the area of the sector?
```
A = (1/2)r^2θ = (1/2) * 8^2 * (π/3)
A = (1/2) * 64 * (π/3) = 32π/3 m^2
```

### 3.3.1.5 Arcs and Sectors – Linking Concepts

Arcs and sectors are intimately linked to trigonometric functions.  The angle (in radians) is the key to calculating related quantities.  Consider a sector with radius *r* and central angle θ (in radians). We can use trigonometry (sine, cosine, tangent) to relate the sides of the triangle formed by the radius and the chord of the arc.

> **Exam Tip:**  Remember to always use radians when dealing with arc length and sector area formulas.  Use degree to radian conversion only when prompted.
