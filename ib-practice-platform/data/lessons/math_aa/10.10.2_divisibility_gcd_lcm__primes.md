Title: Divisibility, GCD, LCM & Primes

# Lesson Overview

Divisibility and GCD are fundamental concepts that can be applied to many mathematical problems. The first step in this topic is understanding what divisibility means and why it's important for counting numbers or sequences of numbers.

**Section 1: Understanding Divisibility**

1. Explain the definition of divisibility by breaking down each number into its prime factors, including the prime factor that can be divided evenly by any other number.
2. Discuss how to find the sum of all prime factors for a given number and explain why it's important for calculating the GCD.
3. Give examples of numbers divisible by 4 and 7 and discuss their divisibility properties, including their relation to prime factorization.

**Section 2: GCD & LCM **

1. Define what the GCD (Greatest Common Divisor) is and give an example.
2. Discuss how to find the LCM (Least Common Multiple), which can be used for any set of numbers that are relatively prime.
3. Explain why LCM rules apply for multiples of 6, 15, and other numbers in a similar manner as GCDs.
4. Provide examples of these concepts beyond basic number theory such as the factorization of 2008 and its connection to primes and divisibility.

**Section 3: Divisibility Applications**

1. Discuss how divisibility is used in various areas, including algebra (e.g., factor theorem), geometry (e.g., Pythagorean theorem), and statistics (e.g., correlation analysis).
2. Explain how divisibility can be applied to solve problems involving fractions or decimals.
3. Provide examples of how the concepts can relate to mathematical structures such as the set of positive integers, which form a group under multiplication.
4. Discuss connections between different areas of mathematics and explore how these ideas are applicable in real-world applications.
