Title: Unbiased Estimators

Introduction:
In statistics and probability, unbiased estimators are used to make inferences about a population parameter with a certain degree of uncertainty or error. Mathematically, this means that if you know a sample mean from a dataset and want to estimate the population mean, an unbiased estimator will give equal weights to all data points in its vicinity.

Key Concept: **Eigenvalues**
In essence, eigenvalues represent how much the variance changes with a change in the mean. When we calculate the covariance matrix of a sample means, the eigenvalues tell us about how much the variance changes when a measure is changed by an amount that scales linearly (i.e., not necessarily square). Eigendecomposition is used to find these eigenvalues and compute corresponding variances.

Example:
For instance, consider finding the average height for adults in a population with 10 individuals measured. Using PCA (Principal Component Analysis), we can calculate the eigenvectors of the covariance matrix as follows:

Eigenvector 1: [32 84]
Eigenvector 2: [56 79]
Eigenvector 3: [110 14]
Eigenvector 4: [90 81]
Eigenvector 5: [162 134]
Eigenvalue: (∂C/∂x)i = -sqrt(λn^k)

Where λ is the standard deviation, n is the number of data points, and k is the degree.

**Notes:**
- Eigendecomposition: The eigenvalues are calculated using matrix operations.
- The covariance matrix represents the variance change when a measure changes by an amount that scales linearly.
- The eigenvectors represent how much the variance changes with a change in the mean (in this case, from -sqrt(λ) to +sqrt(λ)).

**Example:**
Suppose we want to estimate the average height of adults in a population with 10 individuals measured. To do this, we would calculate the eigenvectors using PCA, and then use the eigenvalues to determine the best fit estimator (eigenvalues equal to zero indicate no linear trend). The result could be an unbiased estimator that gives accurate estimates about the population mean regardless of the sample size or variability within it.
