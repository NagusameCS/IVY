Introduction: Linear Combinations and their connections to probability distributions

 Introduction:
In statistics, the random variable X represents a collection of measurements or observations made in a sequence over time or space. For instance, the number of customers at a store during a certain period is an example of an ordered set, while the amount of rainfall on a given day is another case where multiple events are related to each other using linear combinations of variables.

The problem:
Consider three continuous random variables X1, X2 and X3, that satisfy the following conditions:

 - The sum of their individual products equals 1 (i.e., X1 + X2 = X1 + X2 + X1)
 - The product of their squares equals 4X1^2 + 6X2^2 + 8X3^2 equals 4(X2 + X3)^2
- The sum of the products of their individual sums equals 1 (i.e., X1*X2*X3 = X1*(X2+X3) = X1 * X2 * X3)

 What is linear combination? What are its properties and applications?
