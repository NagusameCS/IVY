### What are Random Variables?
In this course, we will explore random variables and their properties. We will learn about different types of random variables and how to define them mathematically using probability theory. This topic is a fundamental concept in statistics and is used extensively in various fields, including engineering, economics, biology, and finance.
### Basic Concepts & Properties
In this lesson, we will delve into the basics of random variables, starting with their definition and properties. We will discuss the different types of random variables such as discrete uniform, continuous uniform, Poisson, exponential, binomial, etc., and how to define them using probability theory. We will also introduce concepts like expectation, variance, independence, independence of events, and conditional expectation, which are fundamental in understanding random phenomena.
### Probability Distributions
This lesson focuses on some of the most commonly used random variable distributions: Bernoulli (1-digit), Binomial, Poisson, Exponential, and Normal distributions. We will explore how to choose a value for a random variable based on its probability distribution. We also learn about confidence intervals in relation to probability and standard deviation, which is an important concept in data interpretation.
### Random Variables and their Properties
Next, we will examine some of the key properties of random variables such as uniform (1-digit), discrete uniform, continuous uniform, and Poisson distributions. We will explore how these properties are used in problems like coin tosses, dice rolls, and stock market returns. Additionally, we will discuss other types of random variable that have different characteristics.
### Practical Examples
In this lesson, you will apply the concepts learned so far to real-world applications such as weather forecasting, financial markets, engineering projects, and medical research. For instance, a researcher might use random variables in modeling consumer behavior or predicting stock prices based on historical data.
