## Triangle Rules & Applications

## Overview

This lesson focuses on fundamental triangle rules, including the Sine Rule, Cosine Rule, and Area formulas. Students will learn to apply these rules to solve a variety of problems involving triangle measurements, areas, and angles.

## Key Concepts

*   Understanding the relationships between sides and angles in triangles.
*   Applying the Sine Rule to solve for unknown sides and angles in any triangle.
*   Utilizing the Cosine Rule to solve for sides in any triangle, particularly when angles are unknown.
*   Calculating the area of triangles using various methods.
*   Recognizing and applying the relationships between the area of a triangle and its sides.

### Sine Rule

The **Sine Rule** provides a relationship between the sides of any triangle. It states:

$$\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$$

Where:
*   *a*, *b*, *c* are the sides of the triangle.
*   *A*, *B*, *C* are the angles opposite to sides *a*, *b*, and *c*, respectively.

> **Important:** The Sine Rule can be used to solve for any two angles or any two sides of a triangle, *provided* you know at least one other angle or side.

**Example 1:**

Given triangle ABC, where angle A = 30°, angle B = 60°, and side c = 5 cm, find the length of side a.

*Step 1: Apply the Sine Rule:*
$$\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$$
$$\frac{a}{\sin 30^\circ} = \frac{5}{\sin 60^\circ}$$

*Step 2: Solve for a:*
$$a = \frac{5 \times \sin 30^\circ}{\sin 60^\circ}$$
$$a = \frac{5 \times 0.5}{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}$$
$$a = \frac{2.5}{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}$$
$$a = \frac{5}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{5\sqrt{3}}{3} \approx 2.88 \text{ cm}$$

**Example 2:**

In triangle PQR,  PQ = 8 cm, PR = 6 cm, and angle P = 110°. Calculate the length of QR.

*Step 1: Apply the Sine Rule:*
$$\frac{QR}{\sin P} = \frac{PQ}{\sin R}$$
$$\frac{QR}{\sin 110^\circ} = \frac{8}{\sin R}$$

*Step 2: Solve for QR:*
$$QR = \frac{8 \times \sin 110^\circ}{\sin R}$$

*Note:* You would need to find angle R to solve this fully. This example demonstrates the application of the sine rule when an angle is given.

### Cosine Rule

The **Cosine Rule** provides a relationship between the sides of any triangle when two sides and the included angle are known. It’s particularly useful when the angles are unknown.

The Cosine Rule states:

*   For any triangle with sides *a*, *b*, *c* and angles *A*, *B*, *C*:
    *   $a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc \cos A$
    *   $b^2 = a^2 + c^2 - 2ac \cos B$
    *   $c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab \cos C$

> **Exam Tip:** Remember that the Cosine Rule always involves calculating squares and applying the cosine function, so carefully consider the given information and choose the appropriate formula.

**Example 3:**

In triangle XYZ, XY = 7 cm, YZ = 9 cm, and XZ = 12 cm. Calculate the size of angle Y.

*Step 1: Apply the Cosine Rule:*
$$XZ^2 = XY^2 + YZ^2 - 2 \times XY \times YZ \times \cos Y$$
$$12^2 = 7^2 + 9^2 - 2 \times 7 \times 9 \times \cos Y$$
$$144 = 49 + 81 - 126 \cos Y$$
$$144 = 130 - 126 \cos Y$$
$$14 = -126 \cos Y$$
$$\cos Y = -\frac{14}{126} = -\frac{1}{9}$$

*Step 2: Solve for Y:*
$$Y = \cos^{-1}\left(-\frac{1}{9}\right) \approx 96.37^\circ$$

**Example 4:**

A triangle has sides of length 5, 6 and 7. Find the angle opposite the side of length 6.

*Step 1: Apply the Cosine Rule*
$$6^2 = 5^2 + 7^2 - 2(5)(7)\cos(\theta)$$
$$36 = 25 + 49 - 70\cos(\theta)$$
$$36 = 74 - 70\cos(\theta)$$
$$-38 = -70\cos(\theta)$$
$$\cos(\theta) = \frac{38}{70} = \frac{19}{35}$$
$$\theta = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{19}{35}\right) \approx 59.4^\circ$$

### Area of a Triangle

The area of a triangle can be calculated using various formulas:

*   **Base x Height / 2:** This is the most fundamental formula, where *b* is the base and *h* is the perpendicular height.
*   **Sine Rule:** If two sides and the included angle are known, the area is given by:
    $$Area = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$$
*   **Heron’s Formula:** If all three sides are known, the area is given by:

    $$Area = \sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}$$
    Where *s* is the semi-perimeter of the triangle:  $s = \frac{a+b+c}{2}$

> **Exam Tip:** Always check which formula is most suitable based on the information given in the problem.

**Example 5:**

Calculate the area of triangle DEF, where DE = 8 cm, EF = 10 cm, and angle E = 60°.

*Step 1: Apply the Area Formula:*
$$Area = \frac{1}{2} \times DE \times EF \times \sin E$$
$$Area = \frac{1}{2} \times 8 \times 10 \times \sin 60^\circ$$
$$Area = 40 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = 20\sqrt{3} \text{ cm}^2 \approx 34.64 \text{ cm}^2$$

### Applications of Triangle Rules

The sine and cosine rules are frequently used in solving problems involving:

*   **Navigation:** Calculating distances and bearings.
*   **Engineering:** Designing structures and calculating forces.
*   **Surveying:** Measuring land and determining elevations.

**Example 6:**

Two ships leave from the same port and sail to two different locations.  The first ship sails on a bearing of 065° and travels 150 km. The second ship sails on a bearing of 145° and travels 120 km.  Calculate the distance between the two ships.

*Step 1: Draw a diagram*
*Step 2: Use the Cosine Rule:*
Let A and B be the positions of the two ships. We want to find the length AB.
We know BC = 150km and AC = 120km and angle C = 180 - (65-145) = 180 - (-80) = 260 degrees.
However, we need to find the angle between the two bearings.
Let’s call the angle between the bearings $\theta$.  $\theta = 145 - 65 = 80$ degrees.
Now, we apply the law of cosines:
$AB^2 = AC^2 + BC^2 - 2(AC)(BC) \cos \theta$
$AB^2 = 120^2 + 150^2 - 2(120)(150)\cos(80^\circ)$
$AB^2 = 14400 + 22500 - 36000 \cos(80^\circ)$
$AB^2 = 36900 - 36000(0.1736)$
$AB^2 = 36900 - 6249.6$
$AB^2 = 30650.4$
$AB = \sqrt{30650.4} \approx 175.1 \text{ km}$
