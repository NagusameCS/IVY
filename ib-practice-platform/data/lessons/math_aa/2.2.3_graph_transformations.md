## Key Concepts

This lesson focuses on understanding how the graphs of functions change when they undergo **transformations**. We will explore the effects of translations, reflections, stretches, and compressions, relating these changes directly to the coefficient and sign within the equation of a function.  This builds a crucial foundation for analyzing more complex functions and their applications in modeling real-world scenarios within A.1.1.

## Transformations of Functions

### Introduction to Transformations

> **Important:** Transformations of functions involve altering their original shape or position without changing their fundamental nature – their *domain* and *range* remain the same.  We can manipulate a function’s equation to achieve this.

Let’s consider the general form of a function:  $y = a f(x - h) + k$ where:

*   **$a$**: Represents a **scale factor** (stretch or compression)
*   **$h$**: Represents a **horizontal translation** (shift left or right)
*   **$k$**: Represents a **vertical translation** (shift up or down)

> **Example:** If we have the function $y = x^2$, a translation of 2 units to the right would result in $y = (x - 2)^2$.  This is a fundamental transformation.

1. **Worked Example 1:**
   Given the function $y = x^2$, describe the transformation that results in the graph of $y = 2(x - 1)^2 + 3$.
   *   **Solution:**
        *   $a = 2$ (Stretch by a factor of 2)
        *   $h = 1$ (Shift 1 unit to the right)
        *   $k = 3$ (Shift 3 units up)
        *   Therefore, the transformation is a horizontal shift of 1 unit to the right, a vertical stretch by a factor of 2, and a vertical shift of 3 units up.

2. **Worked Example 2:**
   Consider the function $y = |x|$.  What transformation is represented by the equation $y = -2|x + 3| - 1$?
   *   **Solution:**
        *   $a = -2$ (Reflection across the x-axis and a stretch by a factor of 2)
        *   $h = -3$ (Shift 3 units to the left)
        *   $k = -1$ (Shift 1 unit down)
        *   The transformation is a reflection across the x-axis, a stretch by a factor of 2, a horizontal shift of 3 units to the left, and a vertical shift of 1 unit down.

### Types of Transformations

1. **Horizontal Transformations (Translations):**
   *   **Translation to the Right:** $y = f(x - h)$
   *   **Translation to the Left:** $y = f(x + h)$
   *   *Note:*  A negative ‘h’ value indicates a shift to the left.

2. **Vertical Transformations (Translations):**
   *   **Translation Up:** $y = f(x) + k$
   *   **Translation Down:** $y = f(x) - k$

3. **Stretches and Compressions (Scaling):**
   *   **Horizontal Stretch/Compression:** $y = f(x - h)$  (where $|h| > 1$)
   *   **Horizontal Stretch/Compression:** $y = f(x + h)$ (where $|h| > 1$)
   *   **Vertical Stretch/Compression:** $y = a f(x)$  (where $|a| > 1$)
   *   **Vertical Stretch/Compression:** $y = \frac{1}{a} f(x)$ (where $|a| > 1$)

4. **Reflections:**
   *   **Reflection across the x-axis:** $y = -f(x)$
   *   **Reflection across the y-axis:** $y = f(-x)$

### Combining Transformations

> **Exam Tip:**  When combining transformations, apply them in the correct order. Generally, transformations are applied sequentially, left to right, top to bottom.

Consider the function $y = -2(x + 1)^2 + 4$. To understand its transformation, we can break it down:

*   $-2$ : Vertical stretch by a factor of 2, reflection across the x-axis.
*   $(x + 1)$ : Horizontal translation by 1 unit to the left.
*   $+4$ : Vertical translation by 4 units up.
