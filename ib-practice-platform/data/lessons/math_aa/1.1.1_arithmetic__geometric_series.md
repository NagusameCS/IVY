## Arithmetic & Geometric Series

## Overview

This lesson explores the concepts of **arithmetic series** and **geometric series**, delving into their formulas, properties, and applications. Students will learn how to calculate sums of these series, understand the significance of common ratios and differences, and apply these concepts to various problem-solving scenarios.

## Key Concepts

*   Understanding the definitions and properties of arithmetic and geometric series.
*   Formulas for calculating the sum of an arithmetic and geometric series.
*   Applying these formulas to solve numerical problems.
*   Recognizing the relationship between the terms in a series.
*   Understanding the concept of infinite series and convergence/divergence.

### Arithmetic Series

> **Important:** An **arithmetic series** is a sequence of numbers such that the difference between consecutive terms is constant. This constant difference is called the **common difference**, denoted as *d*.

Let the arithmetic series be:  $a_1, a_2, a_3, ..., a_n$

where $a_1$ is the first term and *d* is the common difference.  The *n*th term is given by:

$a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d$

The sum of the first *n* terms of an arithmetic series, denoted as *S~n~*, is given by:

$S_n = \frac{n}{2}(a_1 + a_n) = \frac{n}{2}[2a_1 + (n-1)d]$

**Example 1:**

Given the arithmetic series: 2, 5, 8, 11, 14. Find the sum of the first 6 terms.

*   $a_1 = 2$
*   $d = 5 - 2 = 3$
*   $n = 6$

Using the formula: $S_n = \frac{n}{2}[2a_1 + (n-1)d]$

$S_6 = \frac{6}{2}[2(2) + (6-1)(3)] = 3[4 + 15] = 3[19] = 57$

Therefore, the sum of the first 6 terms is 57.

**Example 2:**

Find the sum of the first 10 terms of the arithmetic sequence: 7, 11, 15, 19...

*   $a_1 = 7$
*   $d = 11 - 7 = 4$
*   $n = 10$

$S_{10} = \frac{10}{2}[2(7) + (10-1)(4)] = 5[14 + 36] = 5[50] = 250$

Therefore, the sum of the first 10 terms is 250.

### Geometric Series

> **Note:** A **geometric series** is a sequence of numbers such that the ratio between consecutive terms is constant. This constant ratio is called the **common ratio**, denoted as *r*.

Let the geometric series be: $a_1, a_2, a_3, ..., a_n$

where $a_1$ is the first term and *r* is the common ratio. The *n*th term is given by:

$a_n = a_1 \cdot r^{n-1}$

The sum of the first *n* terms of a geometric series, denoted as *S~n~*, is given by:

$S_n = \frac{a_1(1 - r^n)}{1 - r}$  (for |r| < 1)

**Example 3:**

Given the geometric series: 3, 6, 12, 24...  Find the sum of the first 5 terms.

*   $a_1 = 3$
*   $r = \frac{6}{3} = 2$
*   $n = 5$

Using the formula: $S_n = \frac{a_1(1 - r^n)}{1 - r}$

$S_5 = \frac{3(1 - 2^5)}{1 - 2} = \frac{3(1 - 32)}{-1} = \frac{3(-31)}{-1} = 93$

Therefore, the sum of the first 5 terms is 93.

**Example 4:**

Find the sum of the first 6 terms of the geometric sequence: 5, 10, 20, 40...

*   $a_1 = 5$
*   $r = \frac{10}{5} = 2$
*   $n = 6$

$S_6 = \frac{5(1 - 2^6)}{1 - 2} = \frac{5(1 - 64)}{-1} = \frac{5(-63)}{-1} = 315$

Therefore, the sum of the first 6 terms is 315.

### Infinite Series

> **Exam Tip:** An **infinite series** is a series with an infinite number of terms.  We often examine the *convergence* or *divergence* of these series.

A series converges if the sum of its terms approaches a finite value as *n* approaches infinity.  A series diverges if the sum does not approach a finite value.

For a geometric series, the series converges if |*r*| < 1. In this case, the sum is:

$S = \frac{a_1}{1 - r}$

If |*r*| ≥ 1, the series diverges.

**Example 5:**

Consider the geometric series: 1 + 1/2 + 1/4 + 1/8 + ...

*   $a_1 = 1$
*   $r = 1/2$
*   Since |r| < 1, the series converges.

$S = \frac{1}{1 - 1/2} = \frac{1}{1/2} = 2$

The sum of the infinite geometric series is 2.

### Applications and Extensions

Arithmetic and geometric series have numerous applications in various fields, including:

*   **Finance:** Calculating compound interest (geometric series).
*   **Computer Science:** Analyzing algorithms and data structures.
*   **Engineering:** Modeling growth and decay processes.

### Common Mistakes

*   Incorrectly applying the formulas for the sum of arithmetic and geometric series.
*   Forgetting to account for the common difference or common ratio.
*   Misunderstanding the concept of convergence/divergence in infinite series.
