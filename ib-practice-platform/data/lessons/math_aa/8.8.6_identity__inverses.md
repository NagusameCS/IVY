## Identity & Inverses

## Overview

This lesson covers the concepts of **identity elements** and **inverse elements** in algebraic structures, particularly groups.

## Key Concepts

- Identity element definition and uniqueness
- Inverse elements and their properties
- Verifying group axioms
- Examples in common number systems

### Identity Element

> **Important:** An **identity element** $e$ in a set $S$ under operation $*$ satisfies: $a * e = e * a = a$ for all $a \in S$.

The identity is **unique**. Proof: Suppose $e_1$ and $e_2$ are both identities. Then $e_1 = e_1 * e_2 = e_2$.

| Operation | Set | Identity |
|---|---|---|
| Addition $(+)$ | $\mathbb{R}$ | $0$ |
| Multiplication $(\times)$ | $\mathbb{R}^*$ | $1$ |
| Matrix multiplication | $n \times n$ matrices | $I_n$ |
| Composition | Functions | Identity function $f(x) = x$ |

### Inverse Elements

An element $a$ has an **inverse** $a^{-1}$ under operation $*$ if:

$$a * a^{-1} = a^{-1} * a = e$$

**Examples:**
- Under addition in $\mathbb{R}$: the inverse of $a$ is $-a$
- Under multiplication in $\mathbb{R}^*$: the inverse of $a$ is $\frac{1}{a}$
- For a $2 \times 2$ matrix $A = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$, the inverse exists iff $\det(A) = ad - bc \neq 0$

### Properties of Inverses

1. The inverse is unique (proof similar to identity uniqueness)
2. $(a^{-1})^{-1} = a$
3. $(a * b)^{-1} = b^{-1} * a^{-1}$ (the **shoes-socks** property)

**Example:** In the group $(\mathbb{Z}_6, +_6)$, find the inverse of each element:

| $a$ | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $a^{-1}$ | 0 | 5 | 4 | 3 | 2 | 1 |

Since $1 +_6 5 = 0$, the inverse of 1 is 5. Similarly for other elements.

## Summary

- Every group has exactly one identity element
- Every element in a group has a unique inverse
- The inverse of a product reverses the order: $(ab)^{-1} = b^{-1}a^{-1}$
