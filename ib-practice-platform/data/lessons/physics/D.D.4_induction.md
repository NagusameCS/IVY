### Introduction and Overview
#### Induction
In this lesson, we will explore the fascinating concept of induction, a fundamental principle that has far-reaching implications for our understanding of physics. By examining what happens when an electric current is applied to a coil inductor, we gain insight into how electromagnetic forces can be manipulated and harnessed within a magnetic field. This topic extends beyond just simple examples; it touches on the principles of electromagnetism, which permeates all areas of physics, from classical mechanics to quantum mechanics. We will explore the basic theory behind induction, highlighting its significance in various fields, such as electrical engineering, signal processing, and control systems.
#### Key Concepts (main learning points)
- 1. Introduction: Induction: A fundamental principle that governs how electric current interacts with a magnetic field.
2. Basic Theory: Electromagnetic forces are introduced through the concept of electromagnetism.
3. Applications: Inductors serve as passive components in electromagnetic circuits, enabling us to manipulate and control the flow of electrical energy.
4. Connection to Other Fields: The principle of induction is also essential for understanding other areas like signal processing and control systems.
#### Worked Examples (2-3 examples per lesson)
1. Inductor: A simple example that illustrates the basic principles behind inductors, where a coil is wrapped around an iron core. This setup shows how inductive reactance affects voltage and current in a circuit.
