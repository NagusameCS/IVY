## Overview

This lesson covers **nuclear fusion** in stars, stellar classification, and the lifecycle of stars. Understanding fusion is key to explaining how stars produce energy and how elements are formed.

- The proton-proton chain and CNO cycle
- Stellar classification and the Hertzsprung-Russell (HR) diagram
- Stellar evolution from main sequence to end states

### The Hertzsprung-Russell Diagram

The HR diagram plots **luminosity** (vertical axis) against **surface temperature** (horizontal axis, decreasing left to right).

Key regions:
- **Main Sequence**: diagonal band where stars spend most of their lives (hydrogen fusion)
- **Red Giants/Supergiants**: upper right, cool but very luminous
- **White Dwarfs**: lower left, hot but dim

### Stellar Evolution

| Initial Mass | Main Sequence | End State |
|---|---|---|
| $< 0.5 M_\\odot$ | Red dwarf | White dwarf |
| .5 - 8 M_\\odot$ | Sun-like star | Red giant then white dwarf |
|  - 25 M_\\odot$ | Blue giant | Supernova then neutron star |
| $> 25 M_\\odot$ | Supergiant | Supernova then black hole |

### Nucleosynthesis

> **Note:** Elements up to iron (Fe-56) are produced by fusion in massive stars. Elements heavier than iron are produced during supernovae via the **r-process** (rapid neutron capture).

## Summary

- Stars generate energy through nuclear fusion of hydrogen into helium
- The HR diagram classifies stars by luminosity and temperature
- A star's mass determines its lifecycle and final state
- Nucleosynthesis in stars produces elements up to iron; heavier elements form in supernovae
