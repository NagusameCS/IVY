Title: Forces and Momentum 101

**Objective:** Students learn about forces as described by Newton's Laws of Motion. They will also understand the concept of momentum, which is a measure of an object's mass or tendency to change direction when acted upon by a force.

Introduction: The first lesson in the physics curriculum is designed to engage students and provide background knowledge for learning about fundamental principles such as forces and momentum.

**Instruction:** This lesson begins with an overview of Newton’s First Law, which establishes the laws of motion that govern our universe. Students will learn how gravity causes objects to move or change direction when acted upon by a force. They understand that every object experiences these forces in response to a changing gravitational field and examine the relationship between mass (the amount of matter) and velocity (how fast an object is moving).

**Activity:** Ask students to predict which object will fall faster: a car on its own or one that is pulled by gravity. Students use everyday experience as their guide, then experiment with different objects, observing how they respond to forces described in the first law. Discuss and compare the results, noting any exceptions and potential errors.

**Discussion:** Consider using interactive elements such as a virtual globe to further illustrate Newton’s First Law conceptually. This can provide students with hands-on experience building simple models of real objects that demonstrate their behavior under various forces.

Summary: The lesson provides an introduction to fundamental concepts in physics, including the laws of motion and gravity, while also introducing students to the relationship between mass and velocity using a virtual globe activity and experiments on objects suspended from ropes or chains.
