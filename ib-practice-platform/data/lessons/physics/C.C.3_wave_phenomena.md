## Overview

This lesson covers **wave phenomena** including diffraction, interference, standing waves, and the Doppler effect at an introductory level. These concepts explain how waves behave when they encounter obstacles, combine, or when there is relative motion between source and observer.

## Key Concepts

- Single-slit diffraction
- Double-slit interference (Young's experiment)
- Standing waves and resonance
- The Doppler effect

### Standing Waves

Standing waves form when two waves of the same frequency travel in opposite directions and superpose. They have fixed **nodes** (zero displacement) and **antinodes** (maximum displacement).

For a string fixed at both ends:

## Summary

- Diffraction occurs when waves pass through openings comparable to their wavelength
- Interference patterns result from superposition of coherent waves
- Standing waves have fixed nodes and antinodes
- The Doppler effect shifts observed frequency due to relative motion
