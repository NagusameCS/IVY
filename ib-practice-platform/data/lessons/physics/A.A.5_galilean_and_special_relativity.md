**The Art of Motion:**
*   Galilean's discovery: Galileo Galilei invented an experiment where he hung two balls from his ceiling. The results showed that when suspended from a vertical branch, the heavier ball would fall faster than the lighter one due to gravity. This led him to conclude that the Earth is not stationary but rotating around its own axis and revolving around the Sun
*   Special Relativity: Einstein's groundbreaking theory of special relativity was born in 1905 by Albert Einstein at the University of Germany, where he met with physicist Hermann Minkowski. Minkowski introduced the concept of a four-dimensional space (space-time) instead of three traditional dimensions
**The Speed of Light:**
*   Einstein's work on special relativity: Special Relativity 102
	+	In his theory of general relativity, he postulated that time dilation caused by motion causes light to move slower than it would for a stationary observer. This effect was later confirmed experimentally and named the speed of light as c = 3x10^8 m/s
**The Theory:**
*   Einstein's theory: The Speed of Light 49
	+	In his theory, he introduced the famous E=mc² (E is energy and mass, squared) equation to describe the relationship between matter and energy in a vacuum. This was later proven experimentally using the Eddington luminosity experiment and confirmed mathematically
*   Special Relativity 103: The Theory of Special Relativity 104
	+	The theory describes how light behaves as it travels through different media, such as air or water, to describe phenomena like black body radiation and the photoelectric effect. This is where special relativity comes into play
**Conclusion:**
*   Special Relativity 105: The Theory of Special Relativity 106
	+	The theory provides a unified explanation for all fundamental forces in the universe including gravity and electromagnetism, with no need to distinguish between the effects caused by mass and energy. This is where special relativity comes into play
